SKYROVER is a newer drone brand focused on lightweight aerial imaging and social-content creation. Its current flagship models all sit in the **under-249g** sweet spot—meaning recreational flyers in the US do not need FAA registration, and regulations are generally more relaxed in Europe, Canada, and Australia.

The brand positions itself as offering **"Ultimate Under 249g Mini Drones."** Rather than fighting DJI on professional ecosystems, SKYROVER aims to bring features once reserved for $700–$800 drones—4K/60fps, three-axis gimbals, AI tracking, vertical shooting, and obstacle avoidance—down to the $250–$500 range.

The two main models in its lineup are:

- **SKYROVER S1**: starting at $249, marketed as "your first drone"
- **SKYROVER X1**: starting at $449, marketed as "pro-grade mini aerial imaging"

This review compares both drones across image quality, flight performance, safety, and smart features, drawing on official specs, independent reviews from TechXReviews and TheDroneVortex, and retail data from B&H Photo. A purchase link to the official store is included at the end.

### Image Quality: Great by Day, Limited at Night

The S1's 4K/60fps footage performs above expectations for daylight travel and vlogging. In its [S1 review](https://techxreviews.com/skyrover-s1-review/), TechXReports notes that the straight-out-of-camera footage is sharp, the 48MP high-resolution photo mode gives plenty of cropping room, and RAW support makes it feel more "pro" than the price suggests.

The main limitation is clear: **low light is its weak point**. TechXReports says the S1's nighttime performance is closer to a two-year-old action camera, with more noise once the sun goes down. If you plan to shoot at dusk or in cities at night, you'll need to slow the shutter, keep ISO low, and fly as smoothly as possible.

### Flight Time and Performance: 40 Minutes Is Real

The S1's **40-minute rated flight time** is not exaggerated. Official tests showed around **38 minutes 12 seconds** in light wind while recording 4K/60fps, and about **34 minutes 45 seconds** in suburban conditions with AI tracking active. That is notably longer than the DJI Mini 4K's roughly 31 minutes and is a major advantage for travel creators.

Wind resistance is rated at Level 5, with a top speed of 15 m/s—enough for coastal and ridge flying under normal conditions.

### Smart Features: AI Tracking + Vertical Video

The S1 supports AI auto-tracking in both horizontal and vertical shooting modes, and its 2.7K vertical output is ready for TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts without cropping.

QuickShots intelligent flight modes—Lift, Loop, Twist, Vista, and Asteroid—are genuinely useful for beginners: cinematic orbits, ascents, spirals, and asteroid shots at the press of a button.

### Obstacle Avoidance and Safety

Forward obstacle detection plus downward sensors are enough for most beginners. However, there is no side or rear sensing, so backward or sideways flight still requires caution.

### Image Quality: Closer to the DJI Mini 4 Pro

The X1's **1/1.32" sensor** and **f/1.7 aperture** give it a clear edge over the S1 in low light, backlit scenes, and golden-hour shooting. Combined with 4K/60fps HDR and 10-bit HLG, it offers more grading flexibility in post.

The headline feature is **4K/100fps slow motion**. For skiing, mountain biking, surfing, or motorsports, 4K 100fps slow-mo is rare at this price.

The X1 also supports **true 4K vertical video**: the gimbal physically rotates 90° to use the full sensor, rather than cropping or electronically rotating. For short-form creators, this is the most important upgrade over the S1.

### Obstacle Avoidance: 360° Sensing

The X1 uses front and rear fisheye sensors plus downward sensors to achieve **360° omnidirectional obstacle avoidance**. [TheDroneVortex](https://thedronevortex.com/skyrover-x1-vs-skyrover-s1/) reports that the X1 reliably identifies and avoids trees while flying forward and backward through forested areas—a meaningful safety benefit for new pilots and complex locations.

### AI Tracking and Subject Following

The X1's tracking is more complete than the S1's:
- Auto subject recognition or manual box selection
- Spotlight mode
- Follow mode
- Orbit tracking
- Tracking of moving subjects like cars and skateboarders

With omnidirectional avoidance, a solo creator can pull off shots that previously required a second operator.

### Flight Time: 32 Minutes, Good but Shorter Than the S1

More power-hungry hardware means the X1 delivers about **32 minutes per battery**. The Fly More Combo with three batteries is recommended, giving roughly 96 minutes of total airtime.

## 6. Objective Takeaway: Who Are These For?

The core value of the SKYROVER S1 and X1 is **mid-range price, high-end features**. In the sub-249g market they are credible alternatives to the DJI Mini series, especially for:

- Practical buyers who do not want to pay a brand premium
- Short-form creators who need vertical video and AI tracking
- Solo travel vloggers
- Beginners who want a low-cost entry point

That said, buyers should be realistic:
- The brand is young, and long-term software updates and service networks are not as established as DJI's
- Some early reviews mention slight "jello" effects on the S1, usually fixable through in-app gimbal calibration
- Ultimate image quality, professional color grading, and ecosystem maturity still favor DJI

## 7. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Do the S1 and X1 require registration?**
In the US and most other regions, both are under 249g and typically do not require registration for recreational flying. Always check and follow local regulations.

**Q2: Can the X1 match the DJI Mini 4 Pro in image quality?**
The X1's hardware is close to the Mini 4 Pro, and daylight image quality is very competitive. DJI still leads in color science, D-Log M grading options, and ecosystem maturity.

**Q3: Is the S1 enough for a complete beginner?**
Yes. The S1's 4K/60fps, AI tracking, and forward obstacle avoidance are more than enough for a low-risk first drone.

**Q4: What is the benefit of the X1's true 4K vertical video?**
For TikTok, Reels, and Shorts, true vertical recording uses the full sensor resolution, giving sharper, cleaner footage without cropping.
