In the portable HiFi player market, the HifiWalker H2 is a precisely targeted product. At under $150, it offers audiophile-grade audio format support, aptX HD Bluetooth, and solid build quality. But is it worth buying? Let's find out.

## Design and Build Quality

The HifiWalker H2 features a **zinc alloy body** that feels solid and premium. Compared to entry-level players with plastic shells, the H2 is clearly a step above. The 2.0-inch HD touchscreen displays clearly, and album art browsing is a pleasant experience.

The **ALPS professional-grade scroll wheel** is one of the distinctive design features. Located on the side of the device, it supports rotation and press operations for convenient one-handed control. In actual use, the wheel is responsive, but when quickly browsing through long lists, the precision is slightly lacking—sometimes requiring multiple rotations to reach the target position.

### Notable Design Details

- **Glass back**: Visually premium but susceptible to fingerprint marks
- **Physical buttons**: Play/pause, volume control and other common functions can be operated directly
- **Port configuration**: USB-C for charging/data transfer, 3.5mm headphone jack

## Sound Quality

This is the core highlight of the HifiWalker H2.

### Audio Format Support

The H2 supports multiple audiophile-grade audio formats:
- **DSD128**: Ultra-high-resolution format, suitable for classical, jazz and other demanding music
- **FLAC/WAV**: Mainstream lossless formats with moderate file sizes
- **MP3/AAC**: Compatible with basic formats

### Listening Experience

Testing with Beyerdynamic DT880 Pro (250Ω):

**Wired Connection**:
- Open soundstage with excellent separation
- Warm midrange with natural vocal performance
- Rich high-frequency details without harshness
- Controlled low-end with moderate bass presence

**aptX HD Bluetooth Connection**:
- Significant improvement over SBC/AAC
- Wireless listening close to CD quality
- Low latency, video watching is basically synchronized

### USB-DAC Mode

After connecting to a computer, the H2 can be used as an external USB DAC. According to firmware 2.0 update notes, DAC mode compatibility has been greatly improved. This feature makes the H2 not just a player, but also an audio upgrade solution for computers/phones.

## Features and User Experience

### Storage and Battery

- 64GB built-in storage, supports up to 256GB TF card expansion
- Official claim of 10 hours battery life, actual test close to 9 hours (FLAC playback)
- Supports fast charging, can be fully charged in about 2 hours

### Bluetooth and Wireless

aptX HD Bluetooth support allows wireless headphones to enjoy high-quality music. When tested paired with Sony WH-1000XM4, sound quality loss was within acceptable range, suitable for daily commuting use.

### User Interface

This is the H2's weakness. The UI design does feel somewhat dated, and theme customization options are limited. Looking forward to official firmware updates bringing a more modern interface experience.
